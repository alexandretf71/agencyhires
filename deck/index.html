Challenge 1 — Volley Funnel

Build a funnel
for Volley.

A landing page variant, 3 Meta ad angles, and a testing plan — built the way a performance marketer who can read data and make a call would actually approach it.

Account: Volley (getvolley.ai)

Price point: $99/month

Core offer: AI daily practice + real human tutor, weekly review

Deliverables: LP variant (coded) · 3 ad angles · 1-page test plan

The Brief

"Pretend I just handed you the account. What would you change, and how would you prove it works?" The answer isn't to change everything. It's to find the highest-leverage lever, form a clear hypothesis, and run a test you can actually read.

The Strategic Lens

The page has one major gap: at $99/mo — 7× Duolingo's price — it hides cost entirely and buries its strongest emotional hook (app dropout survivors) two-thirds down. One focused test can address both.

Deliverable one

Landing page variant.

Current page audit — getvolley.ai/lp3

What's working. What's not.

I reviewed the full page top-to-bottom before touching anything. The instinct to preserve what's good is as important as knowing what to change.

Preserve ✓

Premium editorial aesthetic

Cream/navy/coral palette, Fraunces display serif, generous whitespace — positions Volley above Duolingo/Babbel visually. Don't touch this.

Preserve ✓

Strong "show, don't tell" mockups

The in-app chat demos, tutor review message thread, and weekly loop diagram actually communicate the mechanism. Keep all of these — they're doing real work.

Preserve ✓

Specific outcome testimonials

"Ordered dinner in French after 3 months." "My tutor messaged me when I skipped Tuesday." These are good — real outcomes, real accountability proof. Leave them.

Preserve ✓

FAQ pre-handles objections

The Duolingo/Babbel comparison is addressed in FAQ. Good practice — don't remove it. We'll add one missing item: the price.

Issue ✗ — highest priority

Price is completely invisible

$99/mo appears nowhere on the page — not in hero, not in FAQ (there's no "how much does it cost?" item). Visitors go through a 2-minute placement test before ever seeing the number. At 7× the price of Duolingo, this gap causes funnel abandonment and post-purchase regret.

Issue ✗ — buried hook

Strongest emotional angle is buried

"App dropout survivors" is Volley's sharpest wedge — people who've tried Duolingo/Babbel/Pimsleur and quit — but it's a small card two-thirds down the page. The hero leads with a generic outcome statement instead of the pain that makes Volley's mechanism matter.

The change

One lever. One test.

I'm not rebuilding the page. Changing five things and learning nothing is a common mistake. The instinct here is to isolate the highest-leverage variable — entry framing + price transparency — and test that clearly.

🔬

Hypothesis

"Leading with the 'you've tried apps and quit' pain — instead of a generic outcome headline — and being upfront about price relative to alternatives will increase qualified quiz-starts and quiz→purchase conversion, while reducing early cancellations from visitors who felt deceived by a price they didn't see coming."

Change 1

New hero — pain-first, not outcome-first

Swap "Learn a language with a private tutor and daily AI practice" for a headline that calls out the failure pattern directly: "You've downloaded the apps. You still can't order a coffee in French." The page earns the right to pitch the solution — it doesn't lead with it.

Change 2

Elevate the dropout-survivor block above the fold

Pull the "App dropout survivors" persona into a new interactive "Sound familiar?" checklist block directly under the hero. Visitors self-identify by clicking symptoms — turns passive reading into active self-recognition before the pitch lands.

Change 3

Add price-anchor microcopy near every CTA

"Private tutors run $200+/month. Volley: $99/month — real tutor + daily AI practice. Cancel anytime." Anchors against the real alternative, not against Duolingo. Makes the ask feel confident, not evasive. Also adds a "How much does Volley cost?" FAQ item.

Bonus

Message-match via ?ang= URL parameter

A small vanilla-JS hook swaps the hero headline to mirror the ad angle that brought the click (?ang=dropout, ?ang=pro, ?ang=compare). Ad-to-LP continuity is the single highest-leverage funnel technique available without a personalization platform — lower bounce, higher relevance.

Design direction

Distinctive, not a clone.

Matching Volley's palette is the floor, not the ceiling. These are the out-of-the-box design choices in the actual coded build — each has a reason beyond aesthetics.

📐

Asymmetric editorial hero

Oversized serif headline set off-axis against a floating, slightly-rotated phone mockup — breaks the centered-everything convention that makes every landing page look the same. Reinforces "premium" through editorial confidence, not just color.

⚡

Living weekly loop diagram

Instead of three static cards, the three loop steps cycle through an animated active state continuously — so the core product mechanism feels like a living system, not a bullet list. The mechanism is the product; it should be felt, not read.

☑️

Interactive "Sound familiar?" checklist

Visitors click through self-recognition statements that mirror their own language-learning failure. As they check boxes, the page responds with matching copy. Turns the dropout-survivor proof block from passive reading into a diagnostic moment — the most effective way to make someone feel seen before you pitch.

𝟶𝟷

Monospace data-flavor typography

JetBrains Mono used for data-flavored copy (session counts, retention %, streaks, labels) throughout. A subtle recurring visual signal that reinforces "this product actually tracks your progress" without needing another callout box. The typeface does the work.

02

Deliverable two

3 Meta ad angles.

2026 Meta context

Creative is the targeting layer now.

With Advantage+ campaigns, Meta's algorithm handles audience allocation better and faster than manual segmentation. The marketer's leverage has shifted almost entirely to creative diversity and testing velocity. These three angles aren't three final ads — they're three buckets, each one spawning 4-6 hook variants, fed broad into Advantage+. The algorithm finds who responds; we find what message resonates.

Format

Vertical 9:16 short-form video first

Reels/Stories dominate inventory and attention. Static is the fallback, not the lead format.

Production

AI-assisted creative at scale

AI-avatar UGC tools (Arcads, Creatify) let you generate 6 hook variants per angle cheaply. Testing throughput > hand-polishing 3 ads.

Landing

Message-matched LP via ?ang=

Each ad links to the LP variant with its angle param — so the hero copy already mirrors the hook that earned the click.

The angles

Three audiences. Three hooks. Three promises.

03

Deliverable three

1-page testing plan.

Test structure

Sequence, don't parallelize confounded tests.

Running all three tests simultaneously is tempting — but a new LP tested against mismatched traffic just produces noise. The sequence below isolates each variable so the results are actually readable. The order isn't arbitrary: it's ranked by cost-to-change and confidence-to-signal ratio.

1

Hook validation — ad angles vs. existing LP

Feed all three angle buckets (4-6 creative variants each) into a broad Advantage+ campaign pointing to the current LP. This is the cheapest, fastest signal you can get: which hook earns a click? We're not testing the LP yet — we're finding which angle the market cares about. Signal: CTR / thumb-stop rate / hook-rate (video) / CPC

2

LP variant test — winning hook vs. control page

Once a winning angle emerges, split that campaign's traffic between the existing LP and the variant (with message-matched headline via ?ang=). Now the LP test is running against qualified, relevant traffic — not a mixed bag that obscures the signal. Signal: quiz-start rate, quiz completion rate, quiz→purchase rate, CAC

3

Funnel mechanics — placement test flow, pricing reveal

Only after creative and LP framing have a genuine read do we touch the placement-test flow or pricing-reveal timing. These have the highest cost-to-change and highest trust-risk — change them too early and you've burned budget on noise. Signal: test→purchase rate, refund/cancel rate in first 7 days

4

Scale — optimize winning stack, hold a 10% holdout

Once the creative + LP + funnel stack has a winning configuration with statistical confidence, scale — but maintain a holdout group (10% of matched audience sees no ads) to validate true incrementality before fully committing budget. Signal: incremental purchases vs. control (geo-lift or holdout), Day-30 retention rate

Metrics & measurement

The metrics that actually matter — and why.

Phase 1 — Hook test

Top-of-funnel signals

CTR Directional read on angle resonance. Normalize by placement.
Thumb-stop For video: what % watched past 3 seconds. Hook quality, not audience quality.
CPC Cost per click. Use as a relative signal between angles, not an absolute target.

Phase 2 — LP test

Funnel conversion signals

Quiz-start % Click → placement test started. Primary LP conversion metric.
Quiz complete % Measures drop-off inside the funnel — often a UX issue, not a messaging issue.
Quiz → purchase The business metric. CAC = ad spend ÷ purchases (CAPI-modeled).

Phase 3–4 — Retention

Downstream signals (the ones most people skip)

Day-7 cancel A variant that spikes early cancellations isn't a win — it's a chargeback problem.
Day-30 LTV The subscription business's real signal. A "low CAC" win that craters LTV is a loss.
Incrementality Holdout/geo-lift confirms real lift vs. platform-attributed fiction.

Measurement stack — the honest version.

Meta's in-platform numbers are increasingly unreliable post-tracking deprecation. Here's what you need alongside them.

Must-have Conversions API (CAPI) — server-side event tracking. Reduces reliance on browser-side pixels. Required for accurate modeled conversion reporting in 2026.
Must-have Holdout group (10%) — hold a percentage of matched audience out of all ads. Measures true incremental lift vs. organic behavior before you scale.
Confirm Platform attribution ≠ truth. A platform-reported win that doesn't show in the holdout lift isn't a win — it's measurement noise. Validate both before reallocating budget.
Sample size Don't peek. At $99/mo, you need ~50-100 purchase events per variant for meaningful signal. Plan for 2-3 weeks per phase minimum. Peaking kills the test.

If numbers come in soft

What to kill first — and in what order.

The order matters as much as the decision. Kill in ascending order of cost-to-change. Killing the most expensive lever first before proving the cheap ones failed is how you waste budget and confuse yourself.

Kill 1st

The creative / hook batch

Cheapest and fastest to replace — especially with AI-assisted production (new hooks in hours, not weeks). If nothing earns a thumb-stop or click, nothing downstream matters. Kill the angle or replace the hook variants before changing anything else. Do not touch the LP or funnel.

Kill 2nd

The LP hero framing / message-match mapping

If hooks are getting clicks but the quiz-start rate is soft, the LP framing is the next lever. Iterate the pain-point headline, test a different emotional entry, or adjust the message-match mapping. This is medium cost — a few hours of copywriting and dev time.

Kill 3rd

The placement-test flow or pricing-reveal timing

If hooks and LP are solid but quiz→purchase is weak, look at the funnel itself — length of placement test, where price is revealed, friction points inside the quiz. Higher cost to change, higher trust-risk. Do not touch this before phases 1 and 2 have been genuinely tested.

Kill last

The price or packaging

The most expensive, highest-risk lever. Only revisit pricing if all other levers have had a real shot and the holdout/incrementality test confirms the business signal is actually weak — not just a tracking artifact. A lot of "pricing problems" are actually hook or LP framing problems wearing a price-point mask.

Watch out

False-positive wins — sanity-check everything against Day-30 retention

A variant that lowers CAC but spikes Day-7 cancellations is not a win — it's a chargeback problem and a brand problem. Always validate apparent "wins" against downstream retention before scaling spend or calling the test done.

The instinct isn't
to change everything.

It's to find the one lever that's most likely wrong, form a hypothesis you can actually test, and read the data without letting the platform tell you what to believe.
